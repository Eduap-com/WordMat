Examples of equations that can be solved with WordMath:

Here are some examples that show how WordMath can solve different types of equations and how complicated they can be.

Example with general solution of cosine relation

\[c^{2}=a^{2}+b^{2}-2a\cdot b\cdot\cos \left(C\right)\]

                              \(\Updownarrow \)              The equation is solved for C by WordMath. with the following assumptions/conditions:    \(b\neq 0 ; a\neq 0\)

\[C=\cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{a^{2}+b^{2}-c^{2}}{2\cdot b\cdot a}\right)\]

Here is an example of a higher-order polynomial. WordMath has algorithms to find all solutions for polynomials.

\[x^{4}-2x^{2}+3x-2=0\]

                              \(\Updownarrow \)              The equation is solved for x by WordMath.

\[x=-2   \quad\vee\quad     x=1\]

 

\[x\cdot e^{2x}+e^{2x}=0\]

           \(\Updownarrow \) The equation is solved for x by WordMath.

\[x=-1\]

 

\[40=72\cdot e^{\left(\frac{0,\!619}{0,\!22}\cdot \left(e^{22}-e^{0,\!22t} \right)\right)}\]

           \(\Updownarrow \) The equation is solved fot t by WordMath.

\[t=100\]

Here is an example where the two-point formula exists:

\[\frac{y_{2}}{y_{1}} =\left(\frac{x_{2}}{x_{1}} \right)^{a}\]

                              \(\Updownarrow \)              The equation is solved for a  by WordMath. with the following assumptions/conditions:    \(y_{1}\cdot y_{2}>0;x_{1}\cdot x_{2}>0\)

 

\[a=\frac{\ln \left(\frac{y_{2}}{y_{1}} \right)}{\ln \left(\frac{x_{2}}{x_{1}} \right)} \]

Here is an example where a definite integral is included in the equation. The equation is solved for a.

\[\int_{0}^{2} a\cdot x\cdot \left(x-2\right)^{2}  dx=20\]

                              \(\Updownarrow \)              The equation is solved for a by WordMath.

\[a=15\]

An example of a very complicated equation, where WordMath automatically applies the numerical solver:

\[\sqrt{x^{2}+\frac{40000}{x^{4}} }+x\cdot\frac{2\cdot x-\frac{160000}{x^{5}} }{2\cdot\sqrt{x^{2}+\frac{40000}{x^{4}} }}+2\cdot x-\frac{400}{x^{2}} =0\]

                              \(\Updownarrow \)              The equation is solved for x by WordMat.

\[x=5,\!520396\]

Numerical methods were applied when solving the equation. More solutions may exist.
